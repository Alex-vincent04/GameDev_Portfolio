FPS Demo

A Unity first-person shooter demo featuring sprint, dash, and double-jump movement, raycasting weapon mechanics, three distinct enemy AI types (melee, ranged, and tank with shield), turret defences, destructible objects, and key-gated multi-scene progression across four levels.

Movement MovementPlayer.cs

The player uses a CharacterController for smooth collision-based movement. A sphere cast handles ground detection so jumping feels snappy and reliable.

  • Walk: WASD at 5 units/s (animator-driven)
  • Sprint: Hold Shift — total 15 units/s
  • Jump: Space — force 5 units, gravity −20
  • Dash: Left Alt — 20 units/s for 0.2 s, 0.5 s cooldown

Camera & Recoil CameraFunction.cs

Mouse look is clamped to ±90° on the vertical axis. Each shot applies an upward recoil kick that smoothly interpolates back to the resting position.

  • Sensitivity: 100 units (configurable)
  • Recoil applied per shot as a vertical offset
  • Smooth lerp recovery at configurable return speed

Firing Sequence
01
Aim
A raycast fires from the camera centre each frame. The hit point becomes the bullet's travel destination.
02
Fire (Left Click)
If ammo > 0 and cooldown elapsed — bullet instantiated at firePoint, directed toward raycast hit. Ammo decremented, cooldown reset, recoil applied to camera.
03
Empty magazine
UI reload prompt appears. No further shots possible until reload completes.
04
Reload (Right Click)
2-second delay coroutine. Ammo restored to maxAmmo. Reload sound plays.

Progression Loop
01
Spawn & Explore
Player and enemies spawn. Auto-doors open when player approaches within 5 units, close at 10 units — providing dynamic gating.
02
Combat
Shoot enemies (raycasting), dodge their bullets, use dash to reposition. Pick up weapon drops for stat upgrades. Manage ammo — reload with Right Click.
03
Loot Collection
Each enemy death spawns a drop. Collecting drops increases score (+1 each).
04
Key Spawn
After 2 drops are collected, a Key spawns in the level. Player must reach and collect it.
05
Gate & Advance
With Key in inventory, player interacts with the exit gate to load the next scene. Repeat for all 4 scenes.

Architecture Notes
  • CharacterController preferred over Rigidbody — smoother movement, predictable collision
  • Static damage variable on Weapon class — weapon pickups globally update enemy threat scaling
  • Component separation — movement, weapon, camera, and combat logic each in dedicated scripts
  • Bullet lifetime (4 s player / 2–3 s enemy) — automatic memory cleanup without pooling
  • Coroutines for dash duration, reload delay, and destruction timers
