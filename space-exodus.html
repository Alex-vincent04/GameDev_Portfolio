Space Exodus

A 3D space combat and exploration game built in Unity. Players pilot a spaceship through asteroid fields, engage AI enemy ships and ground units with three distinct weapon systems, manage health and regeneration, and land on planets to explore new worlds — all tied together by N-body orbital mechanics and multi-scene progression.

World & Environment

Orbital Mechanics CelestialRotation.cs · MoonOrbit.cs

All celestial bodies simulate N-body gravity using Newton's law of universal gravitation. Objects tagged "Celestial" with a Rigidbody are automatically included in the simulation.

  • G = 1000 (customisable per scene)
  • Formula: F = G × (m₁ × m₂) / r²
  • Initial stable orbit: v = √(G × M / r)
  • Perpendicular velocity ensures circular paths
  • Full multi-body support — moons, planets, stars
// Circular orbit velocity float v = Mathf.Sqrt((G * centralMass) / r); // Applies perpendicular to direction from sun

Scene Transition System PlanetLoader.cs

When the ship enters a planet's collision zone, PlanetLoader saves the current position and triggers a scene load. The saved position allows the ship to spawn at the correct point when returning.

Complete Game Loop
01
Home → CutScene → Space
Player launches from main menu, watches opening cutscene, then spawns in open space with active asteroid fields and orbiting celestial bodies.
02
Space Combat
Enemy spaceships detect the player within 20 units and begin pursuit. Player engages with bullets (Space/Z/X) or the instant-hit laser. Enemy ships stop at 5 units and fire at 1 shot/second.
03
Asteroid Navigation
100 asteroids fill the field, each rotating independently. Contact with any asteroid destroys the ship instantly. Boost (Shift) helps navigate tight passages.
04
Planet Landing
PlanetLoader detects ship–planet collision. Ship position saved. Planet surface scene loads — gameplay switches to ground-based FPS-style combat.
05
Surface Exploration
FPS enemies patrol and avoid obstacles using three-ray detection. They take 1 damage per bullet, spawn explosion VFX on death at 0 HP. Player health regenerates 1 HP / 10 s passively.
06
Death / Victory
HP → 0 loads Scene 9 (Game Over) immediately. Completing planetary objectives progresses to Scene 5 then the End scene.
System Architecture
  • Rigidbody physics for ship, enemies, and celestial bodies — predictable and inspector-tweakable
  • Raycast-based detection for enemy line-of-sight, obstacle avoidance, laser firing, and ground alignment
  • Tag system: "Player" · "Bullet" · "Asteroid" · "Celestial" — decoupled hit detection
  • Static position variable on PlanetLoader — lightweight cross-scene data without a persistent GameObject
  • Coroutine regen — health recovery runs independently of Update, minimal overhead
  • HDR post-processing profiles set the visual tone for space vs. planetary scenes
